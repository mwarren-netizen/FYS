T16 · The reveal

"To give you a sense of the scale —"

T17 · Wikipedia

T18 · The Library of Congress

"The fourth model surpassed the entire Library of Congress — and we don't even know how big the latest fifth model is."

≈ a few hundred Wikipedias of plain text, by the standard estimate — sourcing in chat.
T19 · Everything we've written

"When you talk to ChatGPT, you are, in a meaningful sense, interacting with a significant portion of everything that human beings have ever written."

Let it linger.
T20 · Hundreds of dimensions

"Now imagine doing that not just in two dimensions, not just in three, but in hundreds of dimensions at once — in a space so complex that humans can't actually visualize it, but computers have no problem keeping track of."

One long, slow, continuous zoom from here to T23.
T21 · Every other word

"And instead of just mapping one word to another, map each word to every other word in the corpus."

A galaxy of relationships, not just stars.
T22 · A trillion little weights

"A million words in English, each mapped to a million others — that's a trillion little weights, just for one language."

T23 · Where every word should live

"You feed the model all that training data, and you say: figure out where every word should live, based on everything it tends to appear near, across every document we've given you."

T24 · Something magic

"When you do that, something magic happens. You get this unimaginably complex map of the relationship all the words have to one another. And at that level of complexity, patterns start to emerge that don't just look like statistics — they look like meaning. Or at least the statistical shadow that our meanings cast."

The web of weights dissolves into structure. Widest frame of the piece.
T25 · At scale

"That's what training looks like when you do it at scale."

Grab the sky and look around the clusters.
T26 · The echo

"A geometry that echoes the shape of human concepts."

The names were ours. They fade — and the shapes stay. The spin eases to a crawl.
